# Into the span!

_The following can be set as slides in a lecture presentation introducing the definition of a Linear combination._

The Cessna Citation II flies in the $xyz$-coordinate system. The aircraft starts turning from its current position $\vec x_0$, with velocity $\vec v_0$ and acceleration $\vec a$

![](https://github.com/danivpetrova/math-case-studies/edit/main/book/MissionLAIKA/img/case2-1.png)

```{image} (https://github.com/danivpetrova/math-case-studies/edit/main/book/MissionLAIKA/img/case2-1.png
```

We can approximate its position $\vec x(t)$ after $t$ seconds by 
$\vec x(t) = \vec{x}_0 + \vec{v}_0 t + \vec a \dfrac{t^2}{2}$.

![slide2](./figures/case2-2.png)
```{image} ../book/MissionLAIKA/img/case2-2.png
```

This means that the trajectory of the airplane lies in the plane spanned by the vectors $\vec v_0$ and $\vec a$.
